    • Non observance of the operating instructions can cause death, serious injuries and/or extensive damage to property!
    • Never open the housing or one of the under heaters when the mains plug is connected.
    • Do not use the units for any purpose other than that for which they are intended. Use it accordingto the operating instructions.

    • Touch the just deposited nozzles or soldering tools first after they have cooled down. Place the soldering pen, the nozzles and soldering tools only unto the support provided specifically for the purpose.
    • When the under heater is in use its housing, the exit of the cooling air and the areas exposed tothe infrared rays can reach high temperatures. Please do not touch the housing until it has cooled - burn hazard.
    • Avoid skin contact with adhesives or solder pastes. Contact with the skin can lead to damaged health.
    • Any maintenance of the units must be carried out only by suitably qualified personnel or you must request the attendance of a technician from Martin GmbH.

    # Description of Function

    # Technology

    The supply unit for dispensing, vacuum and soldering pens as well as for under heating is the DBL 06 which forms the basis if Line Rework pc. It is a precise, practicable and portable combination unit for proto-typing, repair, service, small scale production and especially for rework in branch of quantity production.

    It fulfils all your requirements for dealing with SMDs:

    • Desoldering of SMDs
    • Removal of used solder
    • Application of solder paste or adhesive
    • Picking and placing
    • Soldering of SMDs
    • Reballing of BGAs, CSPs and QFNs.

    The SMD Compact Station DBL 06 enables you to deal with almost with all SMD types from 0201, SOs, PLCCs, QFPs, BGAs, CSPs, QFNs, µSMDs to plugs and non-standard components. You only need the required pick & place and solder tools. Even Reballing of BGA and CSP can be realized with the required mini oven.

    Your DBL 06, as component of our Line Rework pc, can be augmented with the optional extras in a modular manner to build up high value and comprehensive MARTIN rework station.

    Exact energy delivery to the soldering point is by means of a hot pen, which uses compressed air or inert gas such as nitrogen. Particularly useful is the ability to quickly change the nozzles of the hot air pen as well as easily being able to clamp it to the magnetic keep of the positioning arm.

    The under heater provides support for hot gas soldering. Your DBL 06 will accept the small IRH under heater (for e.g. Mobile Phones) or one of the areas under heaters HIF.

    This involves the heating of the substrate from below to an uncritical temperature (130 - 190°C). This reduces the likelihood of temperature overshoots at the SMD from the top of the substrate.

    During its development and that of the soldering tools particular care has been taken to take into account the needs of PCBs and components. This has been achieved by precise temperature measurement and regulation and the least possible amount of heat being used and applied by the heating element and soldering tools. Solder joints with excellent reliability are achieved by using the program soldering function (replicating production reflow soldering processes). Precise amounts of energy are carefully delivered by means of hot air directly from the top to the solder joints and from below by infrared radiation to the circuit board.

    The DBL 06 provided you with an intelligent and very precise Dis Module.

    The 'clever' part means that using the equipment could not be simpler. The extensive computations and interrelationships the dispenser takes account of are of concern to the user only insofar as your dispensing device does what you want it to do – precision dispensing independent of:

    • Temperature of the medium to be dispensed (10 - 40°C)
    • Pressure of available compressed air (4 - 8bar).

    # Vac Module

    The Combination Suction Pen has two functions:

    • place SMDs (together with a suction nozzle)
    • remove unwanted solder (together with a solder removal nozzle).

    The nozzles can quickly be changed, by using just one hand and the Nozzle Park.

    # Dis Module

    Solder pastes, flux creme, adhesives or other media can be applied in precisely controlled and consistent quantities with the dispensing pen.

    There is an effective thermal break between the grip of the dispensing pen and the medium in the syringe.

    # Auto Vision Placer

    For frequent and comfortable use especially with Fine Pitch components we recommend the use of our Auto Vision Placer. A high-resolution camera together with 5 automatical axes enables absolutely sure placements of SMDs with the minimum of effort as well as automatic lifting of the components after desoldering.

    With the concept of a PC controlled Auto Vision Placer being realized, MARTIN have managed to tidy up a critical step in QFP, BGA, CSP, QFN and µSMD rework. Rework can now be carried out quickly and safely, for the first time independent from the naked eye, with the automatic Auto Vision Placer.

    # Auto Star Placer

    The Auto-Star-Placer operates with five motorized axes just as the Auto-Vision-Placer. It offers effortless processing and secure placement of SMDs, as well as automatic lifting of components after desoldering.

    Placement is directed by using the Star Tools and the naked eye.

    With the concept of a PC controlled Auto Vision Placer being realized, MARTIN have managed to tidy up a critical step in QFP, BGA, CSP, QFN and µSMD rework. Rework can now be carried out quickly and safely, for the first time independent from the naked eye, with the automatic Auto Vision Placer.

    # Smart Fix Compact 05 / 08 / 09

    For the precision handling of SMD packages Smart Fix Compact is an important accessory.

    PCBs are supported securely along the whole of one edge and by magnetic clamps in almost arbitrary form.

    The Positioning Arm (AVP or ASP) is guided by a straight edge, roughly aligned on the Smart Fix and magnetically locked.

    Fine positioning is carried out with the motorised 5 axes. Fans help the PCBs to cool rapidly after soldering.

    # Under heater – IRH

    Heating a large area of the underside of a printed circuit board takes the Martin Rework Process closer to the conditions prevailing in large reflow ovens. Thus, allows temperatures of the above board heating arrangements to be lowered and PCB distortions are avoided.

    After the soldering process the under heater is cooled by compressed air.

    # Under heater – HIF 08 / 09

    Heating a large area of the button side of a printed circuit board takes the Martin Rework Process closer to the conditions prevailing in large reflow ovens. This allows temperatures of the above board heating arrangements to be lowered and PCB distortions are avoided.

    By moving the hand rest of Smart Fix Compact it can be made suitable for any size of board. The heaters thus not needed, will be switched off automatically.

    In operation the unit is monitoring the applied mains voltage. Any fluctuations will be compensated for to ensure constant soldering results.

    # Installation / Switching On

    # 1. Control of the voltage

    WARNING

    TAKE CARE

    Before plugging the unit into the mains ensure that the voltage selector is set correctly (see name plate). Noncompliance with these instructions can lead damaging the unit.

    # 2. Positioning of the SMD Units

    The unit should be placed on a flat surface so that there is free access of air to and out of the unit.

    # 3. Disconnect SMD unit from power grid

    # 4. Open SMD unit

    WARNING

    TAKE CARE

    Open and close the cover very carefully! There is a danger of crushing your fingers and cables.

    # 5. Remove Transport Lock

    # 6. Connection of Compressed Air

    WARNING

    TAKE CARE

    Please ensure that filtered (40 µm), dry and oil-free compressed air is supplied. Please adjust the working pressure, if possible, to 5.5 bar (higher operating pressure results in slightly lower air flow).

    # 7. Connection of SMD Units to Mains Supply

    WARNING

    TAKE CARE

    Before your SMD Units are connected to the mains, check that the mains cable and plugs are undamaged. Plug the units into the mains only with the units' own mains switches are in the off position. Non-compliance with these instructions can lead to personal injury and/or damage to the units.

    TIP

    DBL 06 NOTES:

    • Because of the big transformer in the DBL 06 unit a fuse of at least 16 A / slow acting is required for the mains socket.
    • For the Under heater HIF 09 (5000W) a single phase 230 V AC, 25 A supply is required.

    # 8. Connection of Foot Switch

    Connect the Foot Switch to the plugs (foot switch) at the rear of the DBL 06.

    # 9. Connection of Pens / Heater / Arm / Camera

    Plug in the pens / heater / arm / dispensing head into their corresponding sockets of the modules and hand tighten the screw retention sleeves.

    When present, the camera of the AVP arm is connected to the scheduled USB extension cable with the PC.

    WARNING

    TAKE CARE

    • Carefully connect the electro-pneumatic plug of the Hot Air Pen. Leaks at the connector can lead to the pen being damaged.
    • Please do not plug the older types of under heaters: IR, IRD or IRK into the connector or the IRH- heater (with compressed air cooling), instead connect them always directly to the DBL 06.

    # 10. Switching on of the SMD Units

    Now you can switch on your SMD units by the mains switches.

    TIP

    NOTES:

    • Please take note of the cautions outlined in the Chapter Safety.
    • You can switch the display of the DBL 06 between German and English.

    # 11. Connection of PC

    After you have installed the MARTIN software you connect your PC to the SMD unit by plugging the supplied USB cable in the corresponding socket at the rear of the machine.

    WARNING

    TAKE CARE

    Please only use the scheduled original cable for the PC connection.

    TIP

    NOTE:

    If the DBL 06 or the camera is connected to a PC before the MARTIN software is installed, Windows automatically installs an incorrect driver.

    # Function

    If you selected the automatic function, the vacuum will be activated automatically together with the hot air (useful when de soldering unwanted solder).

    # Dis Module

    # Volume (0,20mm³)

    Here the dispense volume is set accurately between 0.01 and 10.0 mm³. For Bead Dispensing the pressure is set between 0.10 and 3.00 bar.

    You adjust your dispensing device here.

    WARNING

    • This equipment must never be left in its continuous running mode without supervision since there is a danger of starting a fire caused by the IR under heater or the hot air.
    • Do not touch either the heating element of the hot air pen nor the nozzles nor the glass of the under heater: danger of burns!

    WARNING

    TAKE CARE

    Do not squeeze the hose of the hot air pen. The interruption of the air flow can cause overheating of the heating barrel.

    TIP

    NOTES:

    • For safety reasons manual soldering is switched off automatically after 6 minutes.
    • In order not to stress the components unnecessarily the temperature should be kept to a minimum (e.g. 300°C - 350°C when using solder nozzles).

    • As soon as the unit has been switched on the hot air pen will be heated to a stand-by temperature, while maintaining a small gas flow rate.
    • The unit will switch to stand by (= Sleep Mode) after 20 minutes of non-use.

    # Dis Module Check

    Please plug in the 3-pin plug 20°C / 3 sec into the socket of the dispensing pen and tighten it firmly. Set the volume to 1.0mm³ and the Vol Cal to 5.0. This is followed with pressing the Dis button to start the module. Please measure the time taken by the dispense cycle. It should take approximately 3 sec.

    Please repeat the above with the 3-pin Plug 40°C / 1.2 sec.

    # Air Module Check

    Please remove all hot air pens from your SMD device and plug in the 7-pin plug 100°C in the socket for the hot air pen, tighten it and start the module.

    The display should show 100°C ± 5.

    When the end of the compressed air connection (plug) is covered with a finger, the unit should react with an error message.

    Please repeat the above with the 7-pin plug 400°C.

    Please check the air flows from 1 to 25l/min. A deviation of 1 liter/min is OK.

    # Solder Separator – Vacuum Pen

    The used solder sucker should be cleaned with our cleaning tool daily or when the suction becomes insufficient:

    1. Remove the used solder sucker nozzle together with the cooler from the placing pen and lever out the Teflon-tip from the used sucker nozzle.
    2. Push the cleaning tool into the Teflon-tip and remove any solder and/or flux by rotating it.
    3. When necessary replace the solder separator in the glass bulb and the filter in the cooler too. The cleaning tool can also be used for cleaning out the glass pistons.

    TIP

    TAKE CARE

    When the solder separator is full flux can get into the pen or even into the main unit, where it can cause damage.

    # Placement Nozzle – AVP

    The underside of the placement nozzle must be kept free of grease or flux to prevent components sliding as they are released.

    Please, therefore, clean the placement nozzle with a soft cloth and alcohol daily.

    TIP

    TAKE CARE

    Please do not clean the placement nozzles in an Ultrasonic bath, as this would destroy the white blob (cement).

    # Placement Axis – AVP

    To ensure that the placement axis remains free running it should be dismantled and cleaned once a week. Please use a soft cloth and alcohol.

    TIP

    NOTE:

    Please do not use any oils or grease.

    1. The Placement Nozzle can be removed by rotating the placement axis.
    2. The guard for the placement axis can be detached using M3 Allen screws.
    3. Please carefully pull out the placement axis in an upward direction.

    # O-Ring – Dispensing Pen

    The O-ring of the syringe adaptor should be cleaned whenever changing syringes and a occasionally light coating of grease applied to the O-ring is recommended (grease and replacement O-rings are supplied with the unit).

    # Micro Filter – Vacuum Pen

    When the vacuum pen is in daily use, the micro filter should be cleaned at monthly intervals or replaced (one strainer is originally included with the station).

    To remove the micro filter, please unscrew the internal hexagon screw from the rotating nozzle holder. Remove the micro filter together with the spring.

    The O ring as well as the balls for the solder sucker tips are available as spares (Part No. DB15.0023).

    # Soldering Axis – AVP

    To ensure that the soldering axis remains free running, it should be dismantled and lubricated once a year.

    1. Disengage the locking screw.
    2. Disengage both screws of the magnet head.
    3. Cover all over the rubber ring and the whole bore hole with a grease film.

    # Micro Filter – Compressed Air Inlet

    When the compressed air supply is properly filtered there should be no accumulation of contamination in the fine filter.

    WARNING

    Please always disconnect the mains plug before opening any unit.

    # Micro Filter – Compressed Air Outlet

    When you correctly maintain the solder separator in the placement pen there should be no accumulation of contamination in the fine filter.
